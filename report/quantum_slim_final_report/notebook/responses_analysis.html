In this case, the global energy can be seen with multiple min-max normalization using the min and max among both QPU and SA.

In [16]:
for curr_item in qpu_df["item_id"].unique():
    qpu_max_value = qpu_df.loc[qpu_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"].max()
    qpu_min_value = qpu_df.loc[qpu_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"].min()
    
    sa_max_value = sa_df.loc[sa_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"].max()
    sa_min_value = sa_df.loc[sa_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"].min()
    
    max_value = max(qpu_max_value, sa_max_value)
    min_value = min(qpu_min_value, sa_min_value)
    
    qpu_df.loc[qpu_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"] = (qpu_df.loc[qpu_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"] - min_value)/(max_value - min_value)
    sa_df.loc[sa_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"] = (sa_df.loc[sa_df.item_id == curr_item, "energy"] - min_value)/(max_value - min_value)

Interestingly, we can see that SA samples are all on the lowest level of energy, while QPU samples are scattered across the range of values. This shows that QPU samples are quite bad; maybe it is due to the chain break fraction.

Also, we can see samples of QPU extremely high (i.e. near 1.0). Maybe, these are due to the read errors of quantum machine.

But now, let's compare each individual item.

As we can see with a share x-axis, there are some that has almost all high chain break fraction samples (e.g. item_id=2), while most of the others has samples with chain break fraction lower than 0.1.

Maybe we can try to look into the type of item. For example, the popularity, the type of ratings, the total sum of ratings and so on... Or maybe we can increase the strength of the chain when doing the embedding in order to obtain samples with lower chain break fraction.
